Population

With its almost 215 million inhabitants, Brazil is considered an "ethnic blender" for its immense cultural diversity and miscegenation of races. This miscegenation was largely formed by Indians, Portuguese, Africans, Arabs, Italians, Spaniards, Germans, Japanese, Poles, and many other peoples.

This miscegenation process did not occur evenly in all parts of Brazil. In the Northeast, for example, there were more number of slaves, because of the sugar mills and cane fields. Therefore, the relationship between blacks and whites was higher than in other parts of the country. In the Southeast, the mixture the races was more intense between whites and Indians, because there were few blacks in the region.

And this beautiful combination of arts, religions, cuisine, customs, ways and lifestyles of so many peoples formed the current Brazilian culture.
